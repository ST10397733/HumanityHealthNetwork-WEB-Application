Founders' Story

Humanity Health Network was founded in 2017 by Thendo Phume, Denzel Damari, and Ualinda Siala, three passionate health advocates from Limpopo. After witnessing first-hand the devastating impact of inaccessible healthcare in their rural hometowns, they united around a shared vision: to create a people-powered organization that delivers vital care, education, and dignity to underserved South African communities.

Future Milestones

  • 2026: Establish the first permanent rural health hub in Limpopo
  • 2027: Launch solar-powered telemedicine units in 10 new villages
  • 2028: Train 500 new Community Health Advocates nationwide
  • 2029: Develop mobile maternal care program for remote expectant mothers
  • 2030: Expand cross-border outreach to neighboring rural regions in Southern Africa
  • 2031: Build a national digital health records platform for rural clinics
